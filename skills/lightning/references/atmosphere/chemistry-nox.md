# Lightning Effects on Atmospheric Chemistry (Chapter 15)

## NOx Production

Nitric oxide (NO) is the most important lightning-produced molecule. Regulates
ozone (O3) and hydroxyl radical (OH) in troposphere and stratosphere.

Key reactions:

- NO + O3 → NO2 + O2 (dominant O3 destruction in stratosphere)
- NO2 + OH → HNO3 (nitric acid formation)

**NOx** = NO + NO2. **NOy** = all molecules containing one N atom.
**1 Tg(N)** = 10^12 g = 10^6 metric tons of nitrogen.

## Global NOx Budget

| Source                  | Estimate Range Tg(N)/yr      |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Fossil fuel combustion  | 13.5-22.4                    |
| Biomass burning         | 4-12                         |
| Soil microbial activity | 4-10                         |
| Lightning               | **1-8** (most estimates 2-8) |
| Aircraft                | 0.5-0.8                      |
| Stratospheric injection | 0.1-0.5                      |
| **Total**               | ~25-53                       |

Lightning contribution: **2-20%** of global NOx, but **dominant source in
free troposphere** (>5 km altitude) and over tropical oceans where surface
sources minimal.

## NO Yield per Unit Energy

Laboratory measurements: 9x10^16 molecules/J (Borucki & Chameides 1984)
to 10^17 molecules/J (range depends on discharge type, energy measurement).

## NO Yield per Flash

| Method                          | Yield (10^25 molecules/flash) | Tg(N)/yr |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------- | -------- |
| Flash extrapolation (lab-based) | 0.3-17                        | 1-8      |
| Airborne NOx measurements       | 1-7                           | 2-5      |
| Nuclear explosion extrapolation | 2-40                          | 1-20     |
| Global modeling (constrained)   | ~3-7                          | ~3-5     |

**Best current estimate**: ~250 mol NO per flash → ~5 Tg(N)/yr globally.
Significant uncertainty remains (factor of 2-5).

## Production Mechanism

1. Return-stroke channel heated to ~30,000 K → thermal dissociation of N2, O2
2. "Freeze-out" during cooling: NO forms as channel cools through ~3000-2000 K
3. Zel'dovich mechanism: N2 + O → NO + N; N + O2 → NO + O
4. Most NO produced by hot channels (return strokes, continuing current)
5. Corona/leader channels produce less NO per unit energy

## Transport

- Lightning injects NOx at 5-15 km altitude (anvil outflow)
- Convective transport: updrafts carry boundary-layer NOx to upper troposphere
- NOx lifetime: hours (boundary layer) to weeks (free troposphere)
- Lightning NOx important for upper-tropospheric ozone budget

## Other Lightning-Produced Trace Gases

| Species             | Notes                                       |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| O3 (ozone)          | Not directly; but NOx controls O3 chemistry |
| H2O2                | Minor production                            |
| CO                  | Minor; primarily from hot channel chemistry |
| N2O (nitrous oxide) | Very small production                       |

## Primitive Atmosphere & Other Planets

- Miller-Urey experiment (1953): lightning in reducing atmosphere → amino acids
- Lightning may have contributed to prebiotic organic synthesis on early Earth
- Relevant to Venus (H2SO4 clouds), Jupiter, Saturn atmospheres
