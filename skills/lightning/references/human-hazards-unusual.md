# Human Hazards, Safety & Unusual Discharges

## Lightning Deaths & Injuries

### US Statistics (1959-1994)

| Parameter             | Value                                 |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Total deaths (35 yr)  | 3,239                                 |
| Total injuries        | 9,818                                 |
| Annual deaths         | ~85 (possibly ~100 w/ underreporting) |
| Annual injuries       | ~300-1000                             |
| Deaths per million/yr | 0.42                                  |
| Peak: Florida         | 345 deaths, 1,178 injuries            |
| Peak month            | July                                  |
| Peak hours            | 12-16 LST (2/3 of casualties)         |

Trend: steady decline since ~1930 (urbanization, metallic buildings, CPR).

## Injury Mechanisms

| Mechanism            | Description                                                       |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Direct strike**    | Surface flashover at ~1.3 kA → body current drops to ~5 A         |
| **Side flash**       | From struck object to nearby person                               |
| **Surface arc**      | Current on ground (can be lethal: 11 hikers killed by single arc) |
| **Step voltage**     | Between feet; rarely fatal to humans (current misses heart)       |
| **Touch voltage**    | Contact with energized conductor                                  |
| **Upward discharge** | ~100 A for tens of us                                             |

### Direct Strike Physics (Berger 1971)

1. Current head-to-feet; body resistance ~700 ohm (300-5000)
2. At ~500 kV/m × 1.8 m = 900 kV → **surface flashover** at ~1.3 kA
3. Surface arc: ~2 kV/m → body voltage drops to ~3.6 kV → **~5 A body current**
4. Bulk of current flows OUTSIDE body
5. Body sees: short ~1 kA pulse (us) + ~5 A steady (ms-100s ms w/ CC)

### Step Voltage

V_step = I\*rho/(2\*pi) \* (1/a - 1/b)
For I=20 kA, rho=100 ohm*m, spacing 0.5 m, at 10 m: V=1.5 kV → 1.5 A.

**Four-legged animals particularly vulnerable**: current between front/hind
legs traverses heart.

## Medical Aspects

### Three Categories (Cooper & Andrews 1995)

**(i) Minor**: awake, confused, temporary deafness/blindness. Full recovery
likely.

**(ii) Moderate**: disoriented/comatose, motor paralysis. Burns evolve over
hours. Tympanic membrane rupture common. Long-term: sleep disorders,
irritability, chronic pain.

**(iii) Severe**: cardiac arrest (ventricular standstill/fibrillation). Brain
damage. Poor prognosis, often from delayed CPR.

Only immediate cause of death: **cardiopulmonary arrest**. 75% of those NOT
receiving CPR died.

Burns rarely severe (short duration). Eye damage: up to **55%**. Tympanic
rupture: **>=50%**.

## Personal Safety Guidelines

- **Safest**: large enclosed substantially constructed buildings
- **Good**: fully enclosed metal vehicles (windows up)
- **Avoid**: high places, open fields, isolated trees, water, towers, fences
- **Indoors**: avoid telephone, shower, plumbing, metal frames
- **CPR**: victims carry no charge; safe to touch; CPR imperative immediately

## Ball Lightning

### Prevalence

3-10% of people in thunderstorm regions have seen it. ~5000 compiled reports.
Essentially NO scientific documentation (photos mostly debunked).

### Statistics

| Property    | Value                                         |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Shape       | Spherical (~90%)                              |
| Diameter    | 2-50 cm; median 36 cm                         |
| Duration    | Typically 1-4 s                               |
| Brightness  | ~60 W bulb; visible in daylight (60%)         |
| Color       | Red, orange, yellow most common               |
| Motion      | Mostly horizontal; does NOT rise like hot gas |
| Termination | Silent decay or explosion                     |
| Association | 73-85% following CG flash                     |

Passes through glass windows and metal screens. Occasional acrid odor,
burn marks, hissing/buzzing sounds.

### Leading Theories

Most reasonable (per authors): internally powered, involving material from
preceding lightning strike (tree, metal, ground).

**Abrahamson & Dinniss (2000)**: silicon nanoparticles from sand evaporated
by lightning, oxidizing in air to produce light. Most recent and promising.

No published theory fully adequate. No convincing lab simulation exists.

## Bead Lightning

Channel breaks into luminous ~10 m fragments persisting longer than normal.
Most reasonable theory: cooling time proportional to radius^2; periodically
modulated channel radius → large-radius sections persist longer.

## Other Unusual Discharges

- **Volcano lightning**: ~60 volcanoes produce lightning; both IC and CG
- **St. Elmo's fire**: corona from pointed objects in high fields
- **Nuclear lightning**: thermonuclear detonations → km-length upward
  discharges; estimated peak 250 +/- 50 kA
- **Sandstorm discharges**: ~1 m sparks in gypsum sandstorms
