# Atmospheric Chemistry & Extraterrestrial Lightning

## Lightning NOx Production

NO is the most important discharge-produced molecule — regulates O3 and OH.

Key reactions:

- NO + O3 → NO2 + O2 (dominant O3 destruction in stratosphere)
- NO2 + OH → HNO3

### Global NOx Budget (Tg(N)/yr)

| Source          | Ehhalt (1982) | Logan (1983) | Penner (1991) |
| --------------- | ------------- | ------------ | ------------- |
| Fossil fuel     | 13.5          | 21           | 22.4          |
| Biomass burning | 11.2          | 12           | 5.8           |
| Soil            | 5.5           | 8            | 10.0          |
| **Lightning**   | **5 (2-8)**   | **8 (2-20)** | **3.0**       |
| Total sources   | 39            | 55           | 42.2          |

Lightning: 10-20% of global NOx. **Dominant source in upper troposphere**.

### Production Mechanism

Zel'dovich freeze-out: equilibrium NO peaks (~10%) at ~4000 K.
Below ~2000-3000 K, reactions too slow → NO concentration locked.

Channel heated to ~30,000 K in microseconds; cools to ~3000 K in
milliseconds → NO frozen at high-T equilibrium value.

Total channel length per flash: ~45 km (including branches; Laroche 1999).

## Extraterrestrial Lightning

### Requirements

1. Particle interaction producing charge separation
2. Spatial separation by convection/gravity

### Planetary Summary

| Planet  | Confirmed | Flash Density (km^-2/yr) | Energy/Flash (J) |
| ------- | --------- | ------------------------ | ---------------- |
| Earth   | Yes       | ~6                       | 10^9-10^10       |
| Jupiter | **Yes**   | ~10^-3 to 10^-1          | ~10^12           |
| Venus   | Ambiguous | <=3                      | ?                |
| Saturn  | Uncertain | ~3x10^-2                 | ?                |
| Uranus  | Uncertain | ?                        | ?                |
| Neptune | Uncertain | ~2x10^-4                 | ?                |

### Jupiter

H2O cloud liquid water content: up to 10 g/m^3 (vs ~3 g/m^3 Earth).
Optical energy/flash: (2.5+/-1.9)x10^9 J → total ~10^12 J (~1000x Earth).
Greatest single flash: 1.6x10^10 J (several x Earth "superbolts").
Inferred currents: 10^5-10^6 A (if source at 1000-10000 km).

167 whistlers detected by Voyager 1. Locations: 55-75N, 60S.

### Venus

97% CO2, ~90 bar surface. Evidence ambiguous:

- Venera 9: optical pulses at 10x Earth localized storm rate
- Pioneer Venus star sensor: NO optical signals
- Cassini flyby: NO RF signals detected

### Saturn

Saturn Electrostatic Discharges (SEDs): 18,000 events (Voyager 1).
20 kHz to 40 MHz; power 10^9-10^10 W; ~10 hr periodicity.
Origin uncertain (atmosphere or rings).

### Neptune

16 whistlers detected by Voyager 2; dispersion ~26,000 s*Hz^1/2 (huge).
Good fit to Eckersley law → convincing dispersive propagation.
