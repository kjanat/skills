# Cloud Electrification & Charge Structure

## Cumulonimbus Parameters

| Parameter                  | Value                 |
| -------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Vertical extent            | 3-20 km               |
| Horizontal extent          | ~3 km to >50 km       |
| Storm speed (flat terrain) | 20-30 km/hr           |
| Mixed-phase region         | 0C to -40C (key zone) |
| Cell lifetime              | ~1 hr                 |
| Updraft threshold          | >=10 m/s              |

## Tripole Model

- **Top**: main positive charge
- **Middle**: main negative (~-10 to -25C)
- **Bottom**: smaller positive
- Screening layer at cloud top (negative)
- Corona space charge near ground (positive)

Model parameters: main neg at 7 km (40 C), main pos at 12 km (40 C),
lower pos at 2 km (3 C).

In-updraft soundings: 4 charge regions (tripole + screening).
Outside updrafts: 6+ alternating-polarity regions.

## Graupel-Ice (Noninductive) Mechanism

**Dominant electrification mechanism** (growing consensus).

Collisions between graupel (>0.3 m/s fall speed) and ice crystals in presence
of supercooled water. Gravity separates heavy graupel (falls) from light ice
crystals (carried up).

**Reversal temperature T_R**: -10 to -20C

- Below T_R: graupel acquires negative → main negative region
- Above T_R: graupel acquires positive → lower positive center

Baker-Dash model: liquid-like layer (LLL) on ice; thicker LLL transfers mass

- negative OH^- to thinner LLL. Faster-growing surface acquires positive.

Multiple charge reversals if droplets <10 um → may explain multi-layer
structure.

## Convection Mechanism (Alternative)

1. Updrafts carry positive fair-weather space charge to cloud top
2. Cosmic-ray negative charge attracted to boundary → screening layer
3. Downdrafts carry negative to base
4. Negative at base → positive corona from ground → feedback

**Problems**: Cannot place negative charge in consistent temperature range.
Estimated corona transfer (~10s-100 C over storm lifetime) ≈ single flash.

## Lower Positive Charge Hypotheses

1. Graupel warming below T_R (charging sign reversal)
2. Lightning charge deposition — but observed before first flash
3. Corona from ground carried into cloud
4. Screening layer at lower boundary
5. Carried by precipitation particles
