# Winter Lightning in Japan (Chapter 8)

## Why Special

Two unique features: (1) most CG discharges are **positive** (reversed from
summer), (2) extensive Japanese dataset from last 2-3 decades.

## Meteorological Conditions

| Parameter             | Summer | Winter           |
| --------------------- | ------ | ---------------- |
| Tropopause altitude   | ~16 km | ~10 km           |
| -10C level            | Higher | Close to surface |
| Updraft speed at -10C | Higher | Very low         |
| Cloud base (visible)  | Higher | 200-800 m ASL    |
| Cloud top             | ~16 km | ~5-7 km          |

Cause: Dry polar air from Siberia advects over warm **Tsushima current**
(Sea of Japan) creating convective clouds. Strong vertical wind shear.

## Cloud Charge Evolution (Kitagawa & Michimoto 1994)

1. **Developing**: Positive dipole (pos top, neg middle at -20 to -10C)
2. **Mature**: Tripole (short-lived, <10 min early/late winter, <several min midwinter)
3. **Dissipating**: Positive monopole (ice crystals/snowflakes); longest stage

Reversal temperature at -11C level. Lightning activity requires -10C level
altitude >1.8 km; weak/no activity at 1.4-1.8 km; none below 1.4 km.

## Polarity Characteristics (Table 8.1)

| Study                              | N+ (%) | N- (%) | N+/- (%) |
| ---------------------------------- | ------ | ------ | -------- |
| 88-m tower + 200-m stack (1978-86) | 47     | 37     | 16       |
| 200-m stack, Fukui (1986-92)       | 78     | 11     | 11       |
| 150-m tower, Maki (1987-91)        | 85     | 10     | 5        |

Winter lightning dominated by **positive CG** (up to 85%).
Bipolar flashes: 5-16% (much higher than summer ~0%).

## Current Characteristics

- Peak currents in winter positive flashes: very high
- Goto & Narita (1995): directly measured 280, 320, 340 kA peaks
- Charge transfers: 180-400 C per flash
- Single-stroke flashes dominant (>80% in winter vs ~20% summer)
- Upward flashes from tall objects: very common in winter

## Lightning Incidence

- Most winter activity within 20-30 km of coast (Sea of Japan)
- Most ground terminations on sea surface
- Flash rates: lower than summer storms but more energetic per flash
- Season: October to March; peak December-January

## Rocket-Triggered Lightning in Winter

- Successfully triggered at Hokuriku coast sites
- Higher success rates (~50-80%) than summer (ambient fields often high)
- Triggered flashes show same polarity bias (positive dominance)
- Used for power line interaction studies
