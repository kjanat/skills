# Lightning and Airborne Vehicles (Chapter 10)

## Key Finding

~90% of lightning strikes to aircraft are **initiated by the aircraft** itself,
not intercepted natural flashes. Bidirectional leader theory (Kasemir 1950).

## Strike Statistics

- Commercial aircraft struck ~once per 3000 flight hours (~once/year)
- Most strikes occur at 0C level (freezing): ~3-5 km altitude
- 70% associated with rain; 12% with rain/snow/sleet/hail mix
- 27% local instability; 53% organized fronts/squall lines
- Majority of strikes within cloud (only few % below/beside cloud)
- ~40% of discharges occur where no thunderstorms reported

### Strike Altitude Distribution

Peak occurrence: 2-5 km (0C to -10C temperature range).
Winter strikes (Japan): same temperature range but much lower altitude.

## Initiation Mechanism

1. Aircraft extremities enhance ambient field (~10x enhancement factor)
2. Ambient field in thunderclouds: ~50 kV/m typically
3. Positive leader launches first from one extremity (in field direction)
4. Milliseconds later: negative leader from opposite extremity
5. Field required for initiation at ~6 km altitude: ~1.5 MV/m at tip
6. Aircraft corona charge: ~1 mC (too small for observed damage alone)

## Swept Stroke Phenomenon

Aircraft moves relative to lightning attachment point during flash.
Results in sequence of burn marks/holes along fuselage.
Critical for composite aircraft (non-metallic skins lack conductivity).

## Lightning Test Standards for Aircraft

- DO-160 Section 22 (now DO-160G): indirect effects test levels
- SAE ARP 5412A / ED-105: external environment definitions
- SAE ARP 5414 / ED-84: direct effects protection
- MIL-STD-1757A: military aircraft lightning qualification

### Standard Waveform Components

| Component              | Peak            | Duration | Represents          |
| ---------------------- | --------------- | -------- | ------------------- |
| A (initial stroke)     | 200 kA          | 500 us   | First return stroke |

| D (subsequent stroke)  | 100 kA          | 500 us   | Subsequent RS       |
| H (multiple burst)     | Multiple pulses | --       | Multiple strokes    |

## Notable Accidents

### Boeing 707 (Pan Am 214), 1963

- Struck near Elkton, Maryland; 81 killed
- Lightning ignited fuel vapors in wing tank
- Led to: fuel tank inerting requirements, lightning protection standards

### Apollo 12, 1969

- Vehicle-initiated lightning 36.5 s after launch (~1.9 km altitude)
- Second cloud discharge at 52 s (~4.4 km)
- Temporary loss of guidance, fuel cells, telemetry; mission continued
- Led to: NASA launch commit criteria for lightning, research funding

### Atlas Centaur 67, 1987

- Vehicle-initiated strike at 49 s post-launch (~3.6 km altitude)
- Guidance memory upset → unplanned rotation → structural breakup
- Range safety officer destroyed vehicle at ~70 s
- Peak current: ~20 kA (negative); ambient field: -7.8 kV/m at launch
