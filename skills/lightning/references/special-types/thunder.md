# Thunder (Chapter 11)

## Audibility

- Maximum audible range: ~25 km (typically <15 km for CG)
- "Heat lightning": lightning seen beyond audible range (>25 km)
- Sound speed in air: ~340 m/s → 3 s per km distance
- Flash-to-bang rule: count seconds, divide by ~3 for km (5 for miles)

## Acoustic Characteristics

| Parameter                             | Value                                 |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Peak overpressure at 1 m from channel | ~4 atm                                |
| Peak overpressure at 10 m             | ~0.4 atm                              |
| Peak overpressure at 1 km             | ~0.01 atm                             |
| Dominant frequency at close range     | ~100 Hz                               |
| Dominant frequency at distance        | 10-100 Hz                             |
| Duration of thunder from CG flash     | 1-30+ s                               |
| Infrasonic component (<20 Hz)         | Always present; can propagate >100 km |

## Generation Mechanisms

### Primary: Rapid Heating of Channel

1. Return stroke heats channel to ~30,000 K in <10 us
2. Channel pressure rises to ~8 atm in first 5 us
3. Supersonic shock wave expands outward
4. Shock decays to sound wave at ~1-3 m from channel
5. This is analogous to sonic boom but cylindrical

### Channel Geometry Effects

- Tortuous channel geometry causes interference patterns
- Channel sections at different distances → different arrival times → rumble
- Branching creates additional sound sources
- Thunder from return strokes louder than from leaders (higher current/heating)

### Electrostatic Mechanism (Supplementary)

- Rapid release of electrostatic stress after charge neutralization
- Contributes primarily to infrasonic frequencies
- Hill (1977): conversion of electrostatic energy to acoustic

## Propagation Effects

| Effect                 | Impact                                                 |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Geometric spreading    | 1/r decay                                              |
| Atmospheric absorption | Preferentially attenuates high frequencies             |
| Temperature gradients  | Refract sound upward (normal lapse rate) → shadow zone |
| Wind                   | Shifts shadow zone asymmetrically                      |
| Ground reflection      | Interference patterns near surface                     |

### Shadow Zone

- Normal atmospheric temperature decreases with altitude → sound refracted upward
- Beyond ~10 km, most thunder energy directed above ground level
- Inversions and wind can create acoustic channels (anomalous propagation)

## Acoustic Imaging

- Multiple microphone arrays can reconstruct 3D channel geometry
- Time-of-arrival differences → locate sound sources
- Resolution: ~100 m at 5 km range (limited by atmospheric turbulence)
- Method validates optical/EM measurements of channel tortuosity
- Few et al. (1970), MacGorman et al. (1981): pioneering studies

## Thunder from Different Lightning Processes

| Process            | Acoustic Character                                  |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Return stroke      | Loudest; sharp initial crack at close range         |
| Continuing current | Low rumble; sustains sound                          |
| M-components       | Comparable peak pressures to RS (Rakov et al. 2001) |
| Cloud discharges   | Softer, more diffuse; mostly rumble                 |
| Positive strokes   | Can be louder than negative (higher peak currents)  |
