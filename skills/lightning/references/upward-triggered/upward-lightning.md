## Upward Negative Lightning (Most Common Upward Type)

Sequence:

1. Upward positive leader from tower → initial continuous current (ICC)
2. ICC: typically hundreds of A, duration hundreds of ms
3. May be followed by one or more downward dart leader-return stroke sequences
4. These subsequent strokes similar to those in downward lightning

### ICC Parameters

| Parameter       | Typical                | Range           |
| --------------- | ---------------------- | --------------- |
| Current         | Hundreds of A          | 10s A to few kA |
| Duration        | Hundreds of ms         | --              |
| Charge transfer | Several C to tens of C | --              |

## Upward Positive Lightning (Less Common)

- Upward negative leader from tower tip
- Associated with overhead positive charge
- More common in winter (Japan): dominant type in winter storms

## Upward Leader Characteristics (Table 6.2)

| Parameter            | Positive leaders        | Negative leaders  |
| -------------------- | ----------------------- | ----------------- |
| Speed                | 5.2x10^4 - 6.4x10^5 m/s | Similar range     |

| Branching            | Minimal                 | Extensive         |

## Current Reflections in Tall Objects

- Current pulse from lightning reflects at top and bottom of tower
- Bottom: reflection coefficient = (Zch - Zgr)/(Zch + Zgr) ≈ -1 (short circuit)
- Top: reflection coefficient positive (impedance mismatch)
- At CN Tower (553 m): current pulses separated by ~3 us (2H/c)
- Reflections complicate current measurements; must be accounted for
- "Undisturbed" current: current that would flow at tower base if tower were
  replaced by equivalent lightning channel

## Tower Influence on Lightning Parameters

- Peak current at tower base ≈ (1+rho_top)/(1-rho_bot*rho_top) times
  undisturbed current
- For ground reflection -0.7 and top reflection +0.5: enhancement factor ~1.9
- Tall tower measurements may overestimate CG current peaks
- Berger's data (Monte San Salvatore) may have less enhancement due to
  lower effective reflection coefficients

## EM Fields from Strikes to Tall Objects

- Field waveforms differ from those of strikes to flat ground
- Near-field: additional early pulse from tower current reflections
- Far-field: initial polarity can reverse for tall towers
- Must use modified transmission line models accounting for tower
